alix is a local-first spaced-repetition learning tool. Decks are plain text
files you own; the app schedules reviews so each card comes back just before
you would forget it.

This Android app is the mobile client of the alix project. It reviews the
same decks as the desktop and web versions, fully offline: flip cards,
gap-fill, line-by-line reveal, multiple choice, typed answers, an
explain-style key-point checklist, and a trace deck's predict/reveal/
self-grade walk. Pick a look from an 18-theme color gallery, the same
palettes the web app ships. Progress lives next to your decks as a plain
file.

A new install starts with a small tutorial deck that teaches the app while
you review it. Delete it when it has nothing left to teach you; it will not
come back.

Optional shared decks folder: you can point alix at a folder you choose
(for example one synced by Syncthing), so decks and progress roam between
your devices. On Android 11 and newer this uses the "All files access"
permission. It is opt-in: if you never pick a shared folder, alix keeps
everything in its own app storage and the permission is not used.

Review, including a trace deck's walk, stays fully offline. Optionally, you
can pair the app with your own alix desktop app on your Wi-Fi (paste the URL
alix prints), and the phone borrows the desktop for the tutor, the AI exam
(a trace's compression exam included), turning a tutor conversation into
notes on the deck, and generating a new deck from a URL. The phone talks
only to your desktop, with a token, on your local network; your desktop
answers using whatever AI backend you have configured there. The alix
project runs no server, tracks nothing, and has no accounts. You can unpair
at any time.

Status: early, pre-1.0, under active development. Expect rough edges.
